    Mechanism of suppression of fluorescence induced by fullerenes in conjugated heterocyclic molecules.

Project

The chemical structure of the conjugated organic molecules establishes affinity with their photophysical properties; This relationship favors the characterization of new molecules to determine qualities that facilitate their application. In this work, the photophysical properties of a new fluorescent benzothiadiazole derivative in solution at room temperature were analyzed by absorption and fluorescence spectroscopy. The study of the extinction of the fluorescence of compounds in a toluene solution with different concentrations of C60 and Functionalized C60 suggests that the extinction was driven by a dynamic and static process by effective sphere formation.
